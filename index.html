About Gilgit-Baltistan

The Crown Jewel of Pakistan

Gilgit-Baltistan, formerly known as the Northern Areas, is a region of breathtaking natural beauty located in the northern part of Pakistan. It borders Azad Kashmir to the south, the province of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa to the west, Afghanistan to the north, China to the northeast, and the Indian-administered region of Jammu and Kashmir to the southeast.

Home to some of the world's highest mountains, including K2 (the second highest peak on Earth), the region is often called the "Paradise on Earth" for its stunning landscapes, diverse cultures, and warm hospitality.

Culture & Heritage

Rich Cultural Diversity

Gilgit-Baltistan is home to diverse ethnic groups including Baltis, Shinas, Brushos, and Wakhi people, each with their own unique languages, traditions, and festivals. The region celebrates various cultural events throughout the year, showcasing traditional music, dance, and cuisine.

Traditional Cuisine

The local cuisine of Gilgit-Baltistan is as diverse as its landscape. From hearty meat dishes to unique breads and dairy products, the food reflects the region's harsh climate and nomadic heritage. Must-try dishes include Chapshuro, Harissa, and locally dried fruits.
